👁 Overview

TrackSub is a local-first bill and subscription tracker. We built it on a simple principle: your spending information belongs to you, not us. The app does not require you to link a bank account, create an account with NaRiSystems, or share any personal financial data with our servers.

✅ Bottom line: NaRiSystems does not collect, store, or sell your subscription data, billing amounts, or any personally identifiable financial information. Everything you enter stays on your device.

This policy explains the narrow, well-defined cases where some data does flow outside your device — specifically, anonymous crash and usage reporting, ads shown to free-tier users, and the optional Google Drive backup available to Premium subscribers.

The table below summarises every category of data involved when you use TrackSub.

Subscription & bill entries No Your device only
Billing amounts & due dates No Your device only

We use two lightweight, privacy-first services — Sentry for crash reporting and Aptabase for anonymous usage analytics — described in full in §6. We do not use session-recording tools, advertising-network trackers, or any service that links your activity to your identity.

📱 Local Storage

All subscription entries, categories, reminders, price history, and app preferences are stored exclusively in your device's local storage using Android's standard data APIs. This data:

  • Never leaves your device unless you choose to export or back it up.
  • Is not accessible to NaRiSystems or any third party.
  • Is removed when you uninstall the app or clear app data.

You can export your data at any time in JSON or CSV format from within the app. You own that export — do with it as you wish.

☁️ Optional Google Drive Backup

Premium subscribers can enable automatic backup of their TrackSub data to Google Drive. This feature is:

  • Opt-in only — it is off by default and must be explicitly enabled in Settings.
  • Stored in your own Google account — backup files are written to your personal Google Drive, in a private app-data folder visible only to you and TrackSub.
  • Not accessible to NaRiSystems — we have no access to your Google Drive or its contents at any time.
  • Governed by Google's Privacy Policy — once data is on Google Drive, it is subject to Google's Privacy Policy.

You can disable Google Drive backup or delete your backup files at any time from the app's Settings screen or directly from Google Drive.

⚠️ Google Drive backup requires you to sign in with a Google account within the app. TrackSub requests only the minimum Drive scope necessary to read and write its own backup files. We do not access any other files in your Google Drive.

📣 Advertising

TrackSub's free tier is supported by ads served by Google AdMob. Ads are shown only to free-tier users. Upgrading to Premium removes all ads entirely.

When ads are displayed, Google AdMob may collect and use certain information from your device to serve relevant ads. This can include:

  • Advertising identifiers (Android Advertising ID)
  • Device type, operating system version, and language settings
  • General location (country / region level, derived from IP address)
  • Ad interaction data (impressions, taps)

NaRiSystems does not receive or store any of this information. It is collected directly by Google under their own privacy policy.

🔗 For full details on how Google uses data from apps that use AdMob, see Google's Privacy Policy and How Google uses data when you use our partners' sites or apps .

You can opt out of personalised advertising at the device level by going to Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalisation on your Android device. TrackSub respects the advertising preferences set on your device.

📈 Analytics & Crash Reporting

To keep TrackSub reliable and to understand which features are actually useful, we use two focused, privacy-respecting third-party services. Neither ever receives your subscription names, amounts, due dates, notes, or any other financial information you enter in the app — that data always stays on your device, as described in §3.

Crash reporting — Sentry. If TrackSub crashes or hits an unexpected error, a report is sent to Sentry, an application monitoring service, so we can fix the problem. A crash report typically includes:

  • The error message and code location that caused the crash
  • Device model, OS version, and app version
  • A short trail of the last few actions taken before the crash (e.g. which screen was open)

We do not send your IP address to Sentry, and crash reports never include subscription data, notes, or anything you've typed into a form field.

Usage analytics — Aptabase. We use Aptabase, a privacy-first analytics service, to understand broad usage patterns — for example, how many people complete onboarding, add a subscription, use the export feature, or view the Premium upgrade screen. Aptabase:

  • Does not use advertising IDs, device fingerprinting, or cookies
  • Does not assign you a persistent user identifier — sessions cannot be linked across days
  • Only receives anonymous event names (like "bill added" or "export used"), your general OS/app version, and no personal or financial content
🔗 For more detail, see Sentry's Privacy Policy and Aptabase's Privacy Policy.

🧒 Children's Privacy

TrackSub is not directed at children under the age of 13 and is designed for adult users managing personal or household finances.

We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided personal information through the app, please contact us at narisystems@gmail.com and we will take prompt steps to address it.

⚖️ Your Rights & Choices

Because NaRiSystems does not collect your personal data, most traditional data-subject rights (access, deletion, portability) are automatically satisfied — your data never leaves your device in the first place. Nonetheless, here is what you can do:

  • Export your data — use the JSON/CSV export feature in-app at any time, free of charge.
  • Delete your data — uninstalling the app or using Android's "Clear Data" option permanently removes all locally stored information.
  • Delete your Drive backup — disable the backup feature in Settings or delete the backup folder directly in Google Drive.
  • Opt out of personalised ads — use the Android Advertising settings described in §5 above.
